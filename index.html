George Washington

The first president of the United States of America

George Washington Crossing the Delaware River on Christmas night in 1776

A timeline of George Washington's Life:

  • 1732 - On February 22nd, George Washington is born to Augustine and Mary Ball Washington at his family's plantation in the British colony of Virginia.

  • 1738 - He moves with his family to Ferry Farm in Virginia.

  • 1743 - On April 12th, his father dies. A young George (age 11) is tasked with the responsibility of taking care of his mother and siblings.

  • 1753 - At age 21, Washington joins the military. In his first year, he is assigned to travel to the Ohio territory to dismiss the french troops, after they refused, the French and Indian war begins.

  • 1758 - He is elected as a representative for Frederick Country in the Virginia House of Burgesses.

  • 1759 - George Washington marries Martha Custis, on January 6th. They, along with their two children, move to Mount Vernon.

  • 1761 - Washington inherits Mount Vernon after the passing of his sister-in-law.

  • 1775 - On June 15th, He is chosen by the Second Continental Congress as a Commander in Chief for the army in the American Revolution.

  • 1776 - George Washington and other representatives, sign the Declaration of Independence, declaring freedom from the English. That same year, on December 25th, George Washington helps direct a secret mission, leading his troops across the Delaware river to execute a surprise attack on the British soldiers, who surrendered. (illustrated at the top of the page)

  • 1783 - After he and his troops were victorious in the American Revolutionary War, Washington resigned from the army and returned to Mount Vernon.

  • 1789 - On April 30th, George Washington is inaugurated as the first president of the United States of America. This inauguration takes place in New York City, the first capital of the U.S.

  • 1793 - On April 22nd, President Washington issued The Proclamation of Neutrality

  • 1796 - President Washington decides not to seek a third term several month before the election. This leads to the concept of term limits being discussed at the Constitutional Convention.

  • 1797 - Washington retires and returns to Mount Vernon

  • 1799 - On December 14th, at the age of 67, George Washington Dies at Mount Vernon, VA. He was buried in the Family vault at the same place four days later
